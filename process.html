Common Questions

FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

What documents do I need for import clearance?
Standard import documents include: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading or Airway Bill, Certificate of Origin, Import Declaration Form (IDF), and any applicable permits (e.g., ZABS for regulated goods, PVOC certificate for applicable categories). We will advise on the exact requirements for your specific cargo type and origin country at the consultation stage.
How are duties and taxes calculated?
Zambia follows the World Customs Organization's Harmonized System (HS) for tariff classification. Duties are calculated on the CIF value (Cost + Insurance + Freight) of your goods, using the applicable tariff rate for your commodity's HS code. VAT at 16% is applied to the duty-inclusive value. COMESA and SADC preferential rates may reduce or eliminate duties if your goods qualify under the relevant rules of origin — our team advises on all available duty reductions.
Do you handle transit goods through Zambia?
Yes. We handle transit declarations for goods moving through Zambia to neighbouring countries including Zimbabwe, Malawi, DRC, and Tanzania. Transit cargo moves under bond, and we manage the full transit documentation, border entry, and exit endorsement process. COMESA transit documentation (COMESA Yellow Card, etc.) is also handled where applicable.
Which borders do you operate at?
We have established operations at all major Zambian border posts including Kazungula (Botswana/Zimbabwe), Chirundu (Zimbabwe), Nakonde (Tanzania), Chanida (Mozambique), Mwami (Malawi), Jimbe (Malawi), and Kasumbalesa (DRC). We also coordinate air freight clearance at Kenneth Kaunda International Airport (Lusaka) and Simon Mwansa Kapwepwe Airport (Ndola).
How long does customs clearance take?
Clearance time depends on the mode of transport, completeness of documentation, and border post volumes. With complete documentation, most road freight clears within 1–3 days at the border. Air freight typically clears in 24–48 hours. Delays most commonly occur due to documentation discrepancies, physical inspection queues, or duty payment processing — all of which we actively manage to minimize.
Can you handle emergency or time-sensitive shipments?
Yes. We operate a 24/7 emergency response service for urgent clearances — medical supplies, perishables, operational-critical industrial parts, and other time-sensitive cargo. Emergency clearances are handled by senior staff with direct ZRA contacts to expedite assessment and release. Please contact us directly on our emergency line for these cases.
Do you offer real-time shipment tracking?
Yes. Every client receives milestone-based status updates via their dedicated account manager throughout the clearance process. We provide confirmation at document submission, ZRA assessment completion, duty payment, physical inspection (if any), and final release. Our goal is that you are never uncertain about where your cargo stands in the process.

Three things set us apart: Transparency — you know exactly what you are paying before you commit. Communication — you receive proactive updates without having to chase us. Expertise — our team combines deep ZRA regulatory knowledge with active COMESA and SADC trade agreement expertise. We do not just clear cargo; we actively work to reduce your duty liability and minimize clearance time on every shipment.
